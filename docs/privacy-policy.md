# Privacy Policy

**Reading List — The Missing App**

Last updated: March 2, 2026

## Data Collection

Reading List does not collect, store, or transmit any personal data. The app runs entirely on your Mac. There are no analytics, tracking, or telemetry of any kind.

## File Access

The app reads Safari's Reading List from a file you explicitly select. This access is used solely to display your saved articles and to update their read/unread status. No data from this file is sent anywhere.

## Network Requests

The app makes network requests for two purposes:

**Web page previews** — When you select an article, the app loads that web page in a built-in preview pane. This is equivalent to visiting the page in a browser.

**Website favicons** — The app loads small website icons to display next to your saved articles. These are fetched from Google's favicon service (`www.google.com/s2/favicons`) with DuckDuckGo (`icons.duckduckgo.com`) as a fallback. When loading favicons, the domain name of each website in your reading list is sent to these services along with your IP address. These requests are subject to [Google's Privacy Policy](https://policies.google.com/privacy) and [DuckDuckGo's Privacy Policy](https://duckduckgo.com/privacy) respectively. Favicons are cached locally to minimize repeat requests.

No other network requests are made. The app does not contact any servers operated by us.

## Third Parties

Apart from the favicon services described above, Reading List does not use any third-party services.
